## KEY NOTES — SWEDEN PRIVATE UNIVERSITIES

1. **"Private" in Sweden is different from most countries** — Chalmers, Jönköping, and SSE are foundation-owned but receive significant government funding. They operate like public universities in practice.
2. **Chalmers** is Sweden's #2 technical university (after KTH). Founded in 1829, became a private foundation in 1994 but is still government-funded.
3. **Stockholm School of Economics (SSE)** is Scandinavia's most prestigious business school. Only ~380 students admitted/year. Very selective (GMAT/GRE required for masters).
4. **Jönköping University** is known for its strong international profile — 35% international students, strong business/engineering programs.
5. **Church-affiliated colleges** (Marie Cederschiöld, Johannelund, EHS) are connected to the Church of Sweden but open to all students. Focus on social work, theology, healthcare.
6. **Marie Cederschiöld University** was formerly known as "Ersta Sköndal Bräcke University College" — renamed in 2021.
7. **World Maritime University (WMU)** in Malmö is affiliated with the UN International Maritime Organization — postgraduate only.
6. **EU/EEA Students: FREE** — Even at private universities, EU/EEA citizens pay no tuition.
7. **Tuition fees are per PROGRAM** — The ranges shown are approximate. Check the specific program page for exact fees.
