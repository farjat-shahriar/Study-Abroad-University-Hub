|-------|--------------------------------|------------------------|---------------------|

| **Master (Laurea Magistrale)** | €150–€4,500/yr | €500–€4,500/yr | €8,000–€30,000+/yr |

> **Key:** Italian public universities use ISEE (income-based) system. Low-income students (including many international students) pay very low fees (€150–€1,000/yr). Non-EU students from developing countries often get reduced rates.

## KEY NOTES

1. **Italy is one of the CHEAPEST in Western Europe** — public university fees are €150–€4,500/yr (income-based)
2. **ISEE system** — fees are calculated based on family income. Low-income students (including many non-EU) pay as low as €150–€500/yr
3. **University of Camerino** charges a flat €556/yr to ALL students regardless of income — one of the cheapest in Europe
4. **Superior Graduate Schools** (Normale, Sant'Anna, SISSA, IMT) are **completely free** and provide monthly stipends
5. **Pre-enrollment via Universitaly** is MANDATORY for non-EU students
6. **CIMEA Statement of Comparability** (~€150) is replacing the old Declaration of Value system
7. **Medicine in English** available at: Humanitas, San Raffaele, UniCamillus, plus IMAT exam for public universities
8. **IMAT** (International Medical Admissions Test) required for English-taught medicine at public universities
9. **DSU scholarships** — each region offers fee waivers + accommodation + meal vouchers for low-income students
10. **Working rights** — students can work 20 hours/week during semester
