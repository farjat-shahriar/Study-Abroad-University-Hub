## Detailed University Profiles

### 1. Seoul National University (SNU) — QS #31
- **Location:** Gwanak, Seoul (main); Yeongeon campus (medical)
- **Type:** South Korea's most prestigious national university
- **Colleges:** Humanities, Social Sciences, Natural Sciences, Education, Engineering, Agriculture & Life Sciences, Business, Law, Medicine, Nursing, Fine Arts, Music, Human Ecology, Global Studies
- **Notable:** Korea's top university; graduates dominate Korean government, law, business, and academia; "SKY" universities (SNU, Korea, Yonsei) are the most prestigious
- **International programs:**
  - Graduate School of International Studies (GSIS) — English-taught; international relations, economics
  - College of Liberal Studies — some English options
  - Graduate programs increasingly English-medium
- **Tuition:** KRW 4,000,000–8,000,000/yr (undergraduate); varies by department
- **Research:** Consistently ranked in top 30–40 globally; strong research funding
- **Intakes:** March (Spring) and September (Fall)

### 2. KAIST (Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology) — QS #63
- **Location:** Daejeon (also campuses in Seoul: KAIST Seoul, and research campuses)
- **Type:** National science and technology university; government-funded research institute
- **Departments:** Electrical Engineering, Computer Science, Mechanical Engineering, Chemical Engineering, Industrial & Systems Engineering, Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Biological Sciences, Management, Aerospace Engineering, Nuclear Engineering
- **Notable Features:**
  - ALL instruction in English (both undergraduate and graduate)
  - FREE tuition for ALL graduate students (funded by Korean government and research grants)
  - Undergraduate tuition: KRW 3,800,000–7,200,000/yr (scholarships widely available)
  - World-class research facilities; strong industry partnerships with Samsung, LG, Hyundai, SK
  - GKS scholarship specifically allocates seats for KAIST
- **Intakes:** March and September
- **Career outcomes:** Exceptional; most graduates work in Korea's tech giants or pursue further study globally

### 3. POSTECH (Pohang University of Science and Technology) — QS #100
- **Location:** Pohang, North Gyeongsang Province (2.5 hrs from Seoul by train)
- **Type:** National research university; founded by POSCO (steelmaker); government-supported
- **Departments:** Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Life Sciences, Materials Science, Electrical Engineering, Computer Science, Industrial & Management Engineering, Chemical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Environmental Science & Engineering
- **Notable:** World-class research per faculty; very small student body (~4,000 total); highly elite
- **All instruction:** English for most graduate programs; Korean + English for undergraduate
- **Tuition:** KRW 4,000,000–7,500,000/yr; generous scholarship availability
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 4. Sungkyunkwan University (SKKU) — QS #91
- **Location:** Seoul campus (Humanities/Social) and Suwon campus (Natural Sciences/Engineering)
- **Type:** Public university with Samsung Group investment (Samsung Foundation of Culture is major sponsor)
- **Colleges:** Confucian Studies & Eastern Philosophy, Korean Language & Literature, English Language & Literature, History, Philosophy, Education, Social Sciences, Economics, Business, Law, Engineering, Natural Sciences, Medicine, Pharmacy, Life Sciences, Art & Sports
- **Notable:** Oldest university in Korea (founded as Sungkyunkwan Confucian Academy in 1398); Samsung affiliation provides strong industry links and funding
- **English programs:** Global Business Administration (GBA) program; many English-taught courses
- **Tuition:** KRW 5,000,000–9,000,000/yr
- **GKS scholarship:** SKKU is a GKS partner university
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 5. Hanyang University — QS #162
- **Location:** Seongdong, Seoul (main); ERICA campus in Ansan
- **Type:** National university; historically strong in engineering
- **Colleges:** Humanities, Social Sciences, Sciences, Engineering, Architecture, Business, Medicine, Law, Education, Music & Technology, Sports Science, International Studies
- **Notable:** Known as "Engineers' University" in Korea; alumni prominent in Samsung, Hyundai, LG
- **English programs:** International Studies Division; growing English curriculum
- **Tuition:** KRW 5,000,000–9,000,000/yr
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 6. Kyung Hee University — QS #351–400
- **Location:** Dongdaemun, Seoul (main); Global campus in Suwon
- **Type:** Private-public hybrid; noteworthy for unique disciplines
- **Colleges:** Humanities, Social Sciences, Law, Business, Science, Engineering, Music, Art & Design, Physical Education, Oriental Medicine, Medicine, Pharmacy, Nursing, Hotel & Tourism, Dentistry, Korean Studies
- **Notable:** Only Korean university offering both Western Medicine and Oriental Medicine (Traditional Korean Medicine); strong international focus with "Peace" campus motto
- **Oriental Medicine:** Highly sought by students from South/Southeast Asia
- **English programs:** College of International Studies; several English-track departments
- **Tuition:** KRW 5,000,000–8,500,000/yr
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 7. Pusan National University (PNU) — QS #401–450

- **Colleges:** Humanities, Social Sciences, Natural Sciences, Engineering, Agriculture & Life Sciences, Business, Medicine, Dentistry, Law, Education, Art & Physical Education, Nanoscience & Technology

### 8. Chonnam National University

## TOPIK (Test of Proficiency in Korean)

> **Required for Korean-taught programs; increasingly useful for scholarship applications**

| Level | Equivalent | When Required |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| TOPIK I — Level 1 | Basic | Not sufficient for degree programs |
| TOPIK I — Level 2 | Elementary | Not sufficient for degree programs |
| TOPIK II — Level 3 | Intermediate | Minimum for undergraduate admission (Korean-taught) |
| TOPIK II — Level 4 | Upper-Intermediate | Recommended for undergraduate; minimum for some graduate programs |
| TOPIK II — Level 5 | Advanced | Recommended for graduate programs in Korean |
| TOPIK II — Level 6 | Near-native | Required for competitive programs; Korean-medium PhD |

- **Test dates:** Offered multiple times per year; available in Bangladesh, Pakistan, India
- **Test centres Bangladesh:** Dhaka (Korean Cultural Center); organized by NIIED
- **Test centres Pakistan:** Karachi, Islamabad, Lahore (Korean Cultural Center network)
- **Test centres India:** Delhi, Kolkata, Mumbai, Chennai, Pune, Hyderabad, Bangalore
- **Registration:** Through TOPIK website (topik.go.kr) or Korean Cultural Center

## Admission Requirements

### General Requirements (All Public Universities)

**For Bachelor's Degree:**
- 12 years of formal education (equivalent to Korean CSAT standard)
- English-taught programs: IELTS 5.5–6.5 or TOEFL iBT 80–100
- Korean-taught programs: TOPIK Level 3–4 minimum; Level 5–6 preferred
- Academic transcripts and secondary school completion certificate
- Some universities: Online/written entrance exam + interview (especially for competitive programs)
- GKS application materials if applying for scholarship

**For Master's Degree:**
- Bachelor's degree (16-year education equivalent)
- IELTS 6.0–6.5 (English programs) or TOPIK Level 4–5 (Korean programs)
- Research proposal (for research-focused programs)
- Contact with supervisor recommended (especially for science/engineering at KAIST, POSTECH)
- Recommendation letters (2–3)
- Statement of Purpose / Study plan

### Country-Specific Requirements

#### Bangladesh 🇧🇩

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **HSC:** GPA 4.0–5.0 out of 5.0 (top universities SNU, KAIST require 4.5–5.0)
- **SSC:** Also evaluated
- **IELTS:** 5.5–6.0 for English programs; take at IDP or British Council Bangladesh
- **TOPIK:** Not required for English-taught programs; strongly recommended if applying for Korean-taught or scholarship programs
- **GKS (Government of Korea Scholarship) — strongly recommended:**
  - Embassy track: Apply through South Korean Embassy in Dhaka (deadline typically September–October each year)
  - University track: Apply directly through individual universities
  - GKS includes: Korean language training (1 year) + full tuition + KRW 900,000/mo stipend + airfare + settlement allowance + health insurance
  - Quota: Bangladesh has annual GKS quota allocation
- **Documents:** SSC and HSC transcripts (attested and apostilled), SOP, recommendation letters, language certificates, personal health statement

**Master's Admission:**
- **4-year honours degree** from recognized Bangladesh university
- **CGPA:** 3.0–3.5 preferred; KAIST/POSTECH prefer 3.5+
- **IELTS:** 6.0–6.5 for English programs; TOPIK 4–5 for Korean programs
- **Research paper/publication:** Advantageous (especially for KAIST/POSTECH/SNU)
- **Supervisor contact:** Essential for KAIST and POSTECH; email professors with research proposal

**Student Visa:**
- D-2 Student Visa for degree programs; D-4 for language training
- GKS scholars: Visa arranged through NIIED and Korean Embassy Dhaka
- Self-funded: Certificate of Admission + proof of funds (KRW 20,000,000+ for self-funded)
- Health insurance: Required (provided free for GKS scholars)
- Registration: Must register with local immigration office (Immigration Registration Card)

## General Notes

- **SKY Universities:** Seoul National University (S), Korea University (K, private), Yonsei University (Y, private) — collectively the most prestigious trio in Korea; equivalent in cultural significance to Oxbridge or Ivy League
- **KAIST tuition policy:** ALL graduate students at KAIST receive free tuition (covered by government + research grants); this is unique and highly valuable
- **English medium growing:** Korea is rapidly expanding English-taught programs; most top universities now offer full degree programs in English at graduate level and some at undergraduate level
- **Work rights:** International students (D-2 visa) can work part-time (up to 20 hrs/week) during study; full-time during vacation with additional permission
- **Post-graduation:** Korea offers D-10 Job Seeker Visa for graduates; Korean companies actively recruit international graduates, especially in tech/engineering; Korean proficiency greatly helps
- **Culture and society:** Korea is culturally Confucian; hierarchical academic environment; respectful communication with professors is important; vibrant pop culture (K-pop, K-drama) makes Korea very attractive to young students
- **Safety:** Korea is extremely safe; low crime rate; excellent public transportation
- **Food:** Diverse Korean cuisine; halal options available in Seoul (Itaewon area, university cafeterias); not as widely available in smaller cities — important consideration for Muslim students
- **GKS competition:** Very competitive; typical acceptance rate is 5–15%; strong academic record, English/Korean proficiency, and well-written SOP are critical
- **Academic calendar:** Spring semester starts March; Fall semester starts September; most universities accept either semester
- **Korean language:** Even for English programs, learning basic Korean improves daily life significantly; Korean language courses are free or subsidized at all universities; GKS includes 1 full year of Korean language training before degree program begins
