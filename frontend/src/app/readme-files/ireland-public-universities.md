## GENERAL NOTES

1. **Free Fees Initiative**: Irish government pays tuition for qualifying EU students in undergraduate programs — students only pay €3,000/yr Student Contribution Levy. Non-EU students pay full fees.
2. **CAO (Central Applications Office)**: Undergraduate application system for Irish Leaving Cert and some international students. Most international (non-EU) students apply directly to university international offices.
3. **NFQ Framework**: Irish qualifications use the National Framework of Qualifications (NFQ). Bachelor's = Level 8, Master's = Level 9, PhD = Level 10.
4. **English Medium**: All teaching at public Irish universities is in English — no language tests for Irish citizens but IELTS/equivalent mandatory for non-EU international students.
5. **Work Rights**: Non-EU students can work 20 hrs/week during term time and 40 hrs/week during holiday periods (June–September, Christmas, Easter).
6. **Post-Study Work Visa**: After completing a degree in Ireland, eligible for a Stay Back Permission of 1–2 years (1 year for Bachelor's, 2 years for Master's/PhD) to seek employment.
7. **RCSI**: Royal College of Surgeons is technically a private independent institution but is state-aided and Ireland's leading medical school. Its medical programs are among the most prestigious in the English-speaking world.
8. **Technological Universities**: TU Dublin, TUS, MTU, ATU, and SETU were formed by merging older Institutes of Technology — they offer full degree programs at Bachelor's and Master's level.
9. **Visa Financial Requirement**: Students must demonstrate €10,000/yr (approx ৳1,280,000) in bank statements when applying for an Irish study visa.
10. **Health Insurance**: Compulsory for all international students — budget €500–€800/yr.
