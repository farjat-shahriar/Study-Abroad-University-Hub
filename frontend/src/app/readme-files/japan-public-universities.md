## Detailed University Profiles

### 1. University of Tokyo (UTokyo) — QS #28
- **Location:** Hongo campus, Bunkyo, Tokyo
- **Type:** Japan's most prestigious national university (imperial university)
- **Faculties/Schools:** Engineering, Science, Law, Medicine, Economics, Arts & Sciences, Agriculture, Education, Pharmaceutical Sciences
- **Notable:** Japan's top-ranked university; world-class research in all disciplines
- **English programs:**
  - PEAK (Programs in English at Komaba) — Arts & Sciences, Social Sciences
  - GPEM, GSFS — graduate programs fully in English
  - Many graduate schools now offer English-track options
- **Tuition:** JPY 535,800/yr (standard national university fee); admission fee JPY 282,000
- **Application:** MEXT Scholarship applicants apply through embassy; direct applicants through UTokyo's own exams
- **Intakes:** April (main); October (some graduate programs)

## Examination for Japanese University Admission (EJU)

> **Important:** International students applying for undergraduate programs at Japanese national universities typically must take the EJU (日本留学試験).

- **What it tests:** Japanese language (if applying to Japanese-taught programs), science subjects (Physics, Chemistry, Biology), Mathematics, Japan and the World
- **Administered by:** JASSO (Japan Student Services Organization)
- **Offered:** June and November each year
- **Test centres:** Major cities in Bangladesh, Pakistan, India, and many other countries
- **Cost:** Varies by country (~USD 50–80 equivalent)
- **English programs:** EJU may still be required even for English-taught programs; check individual university requirements
- **Website:** [jasso.or.jp/en/study_j/eju](https://www.jasso.or.jp/en/study_j/eju/)

## JLPT (Japanese Language Proficiency Test)

- **For Japanese-taught programs:** JLPT N2 (minimum) or N1 (preferred) required
- **For most graduate programs:** N2 or N1 depending on research language
- **English-taught programs:** JLPT not required; IELTS/TOEFL instead
- **Test centres:** Dhaka (Bangladesh), Karachi/Lahore/Islamabad (Pakistan), major Indian cities

## Admission Requirements

### General Requirements (All National Universities)

**For Bachelor's Degree (Undergraduate):**
- Completion of 12 years of formal education (equivalent to Japanese upper secondary school)
- EJU (Examination for Japanese University Admission)
- For Japanese-taught programs: JLPT N2 or higher (N1 preferred)
- For English-taught programs: IELTS 5.5–6.5 or TOEFL iBT 72–100
- University entrance exam (each university conducts its own additional exam)
- Some universities use CEFR language assessment
- Academic transcripts and certificates

**For Master's Degree (Graduate):**
- Relevant bachelor's degree (or equivalent 16-year education)
- Research proposal (research plan document)
- Contact with and agreement from a faculty supervisor (critical step)
- IELTS 6.0–7.0 for English-taught; JLPT N2/N1 for Japanese-taught
- Written exam and/or oral interview
- Portfolio (for arts/design programs)
- Letters of recommendation (typically 2)

### Country-Specific Requirements

#### Bangladesh 🇧🇩

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **HSC:** GPA 4.5–5.0 out of 5.0 (top national universities are very competitive)
- **EJU:** Required for most undergraduate programs; test available in Dhaka (June and November at JASSO-authorised centre)
- **JLPT N2/N1:** For Japanese-medium programs; Japanese language school in Bangladesh or self-study needed
- **English programs (recommended path):** IELTS 5.5–6.5; available through UTokyo PEAK, Nagoya G30, Tohoku English programs
- **MEXT Scholarship applicants:** Apply through Japanese Embassy in Dhaka (deadline typically May–June each year)
- **Documents:** HSC and SSC transcripts (attested by Bangladesh Board), passport, recommendation letters, SOP/study plan, EJU score report

**Master's Admission:**
- **4-year honours degree** from recognized Bangladesh university (BUET, DU, BRAC, NSU etc.)
- **CGPA:** 3.0/4.0 or equivalent (competitive universities may require higher)
- **Supervisor contact:** CRUCIAL — contact the professor whose research aligns with yours BEFORE applying; send a well-prepared research proposal
- **IELTS:** 6.0–6.5 for English graduate programs; JLPT N2/N1 for Japanese-medium
- **MEXT Research Student:** Many Bangladeshi students arrive first as Research Students under MEXT; can later apply for regular master's/PhD enrollment
- **Processing time:** 6–12 months; start early

**Student Visa for Bangladeshi Students:**
- College of Destination Notification (COE — Certificate of Eligibility) issued by Japanese Immigration
- Apply for student visa at Embassy of Japan in Dhaka using COE
- MEXT scholars: visa arranged through scholarship process
- Self-funded: Proof of financial support (JPY 1,500,000+ for first year)
- Police clearance from Bangladesh (attested by Ministry of Foreign Affairs)
- Health certificate from approved physician
- Airport: Arrive at Narita, Haneda (Tokyo), or Kansai (Osaka) International Airport

#### Pakistan 🇵🇰

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **FSc / A-levels / IB:** 80%+ in FSc; or A-levels with AAB/ABB
- **EJU:** Available at test centres in Karachi, Lahore, Islamabad
- **JLPT:** N2 for Japanese programs; test available in Pakistan
- **English programs:** IELTS 5.5–6.5 for English-medium undergraduate tracks
- **MEXT Scholarship:** Through Embassy of Japan Islamabad; highly competitive; contact embassy directly for current cycle deadlines

**Master's Admission:**
- **16-year education:** 4-year BS or equivalent

#### India 🇮🇳

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **Class 12:** 80%+ from CBSE/State Boards
- **EJU:** Available in several Indian cities (Chennai, Hyderabad, Delhi, Kolkata, etc.)
- **IELTS:** 5.5–6.5 for English tracks

**Master's Admission:**

## General Notes

- **Tuition uniformity:** All national universities charge the same standard annual fee of JPY 535,800 — no difference based on program or nationality
- **English programs expanding:** Japan launched the G30 initiative to attract international students; now many universities offer undergraduate and graduate programs fully in English — check each university's English program list
- **Research culture:** Japanese graduate education is highly research-focused; supervisor relationship is crucial and hierarchical; approach potential supervisors respectfully and with a well-prepared research plan
- **Work rights:** Student visa allows part-time work up to 28 hours/week; permission required from Immigration (permission card issued with residence card)
- **Post-study:** Japan is easing rules for international graduates to stay and work; HSFP (Highly Skilled Foreign Professional) visa pathway exists
- **Culture shock:** Japan has a very distinct culture; language barrier for daily life even in cities; learning basic Japanese strongly recommended for all international students regardless of program language
- **Safety:** Japan is consistently among the world's safest countries; very low crime rate
- **Natural disasters:** Japan has earthquakes, typhoons; universities have emergency protocols
- **MEXT vs self-funded:** MEXT scholars cover all costs; self-funded students need significant savings (minimum JPY 2,000,000+ for first year)
- **Academic calendar:** Japanese academic year starts April; October intake increasingly common for international programs
