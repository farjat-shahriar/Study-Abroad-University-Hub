## UNIVERSITY PROFILES

### 1. Cardiff University
- **Type:** Research-intensive, Russell Group member
- **Founded:** 1883 (Royal Charter 1884)
- **Students:** ~33,000 (incl. ~8,500 international)
- **Key Faculties:** Medicine & Health Sciences, Law & Politics, Engineering, Architecture, Business (Cardiff Business School), Arts & Humanities
- **Notable Programs:** Medicine (MBBCh – highly competitive), LLB Law, Architecture, Engineering (MEng/BEng), Politics & International Relations
- **Campus:** Cathays Park and Heath Park, central Cardiff
- **Research Excellence:** REF 2021 — 84% research rated 'world-leading' or 'internationally excellent'
- **Notable:** Wales' only Russell Group university; ranked in global top 200

### 2. Swansea University
- **Type:** Research university
- **Founded:** 1920
- **Students:** ~22,000 (incl. ~6,000 international)
- **Key Faculties:** Science & Engineering, Medicine, Management School, Arts & Humanities, Social Sciences
- **Notable Programs:** Computer Science, Aerospace Engineering, Medicine, Business Management, Human Geography
- **Campus:** Bay Campus (science/engineering/business) and Singleton Campus (arts/humanities/medicine)
- **Research Excellence:** REF 2021 — 78% research rated world-leading or internationally excellent

### 3. Aberystwyth University
- **Type:** University with research focus
- **Founded:** 1872 (one of the oldest in Wales)
- **Students:** ~8,500 (incl. ~1,600 international)
- **Key Faculties:** International Politics, Law, Computer Science, Agriculture & Environmental Sciences, Arts
- **Notable Programs:** International Politics (top-ranked in UK), Law, Countryside & Environment, Welsh Studies
- **Campus:** Penglais campus overlooking Cardigan Bay
- **Special Note:** Home to the Department of International Politics, among the oldest and most prestigious globally

### 4. Bangor University
- **Type:** Teaching and research university
- **Founded:** 1884
- **Students:** ~10,000 (incl. ~2,000 international)
- **Key Faculties:** Natural Sciences, Ocean Sciences, Psychology, Business, Education, Arts
- **Notable Programs:** Marine Biology & Oceanography, Psychology, Welsh Studies, Environmental Science
- **Campus:** Main Bangor campus in North Wales, near Snowdonia and the Menai Strait
- **Special Note:** One of the leading centres for Welsh-medium education in Wales

### 5. Cardiff Metropolitan University
- **Type:** Teaching-focused modern university
- **Founded:** 1865 (as Cardiff School of Art), university status 2011
- **Students:** ~12,000 (incl. ~3,000 international)
- **Key Faculties:** Art & Design, Health Sciences, Sport & Health, Education, Management
- **Notable Programs:** Art & Design (Fashion, Graphic Design, Fine Art), Nursing & Midwifery, Sport Science, PGCE Education
- **Campus:** Llandaff and Cardiff School of Management campuses

### 6. University of South Wales (USW)
- **Type:** Teaching-focused modern university
- **Founded:** 1992 (as Polytechnic of Wales), merged 2013
- **Students:** ~16,000 (incl. ~2,500 international)
- **Key Faculties:** Engineering, Creative Industries, Nursing & Midwifery, Business, Law, Computing
- **Notable Programs:** Aerospace Engineering, Film & TV, Nursing, Law, Policing & Forensic Science
- **Campuses:** Treforest (main), Newport City, Cardiff ATRiuM

### 7. University of Wales Trinity Saint David (UWTSD)
- **Type:** Teaching university with federated structure
- **Founded:** 1822 (St David's College Lampeter — oldest in Wales), merged 2010–2013
- **Students:** ~11,000
- **Key Faculties:** Education, Art & Design, Theology & Religious Studies, Business, Humanities
- **Campuses:** Swansea (SA1), Carmarthen, Lampeter, Cardiff
- **Special Note:** Historic Lampeter campus is the oldest degree-awarding institution in Wales

### 8. Wrexham University
- **Type:** Teaching-focused modern university
- **Founded:** 1887, university status 2023
- **Students:** ~6,500 (incl. ~800 international)
- **Key Faculties:** Engineering & Science, Creative Arts, Computing, Health & Social Care, Business
- **Notable Programs:** Engineering, Computing, Creative Arts, Forensic Science, Nursing
- **Campus:** Wrexham (North Wales), close to Manchester and Chester

## GENERAL NOTES

1. **UCAS Application:** All undergraduate applications in Wales (and all UK) go through UCAS — https://www.ucas.com. Apply by January 29 (most courses) or October 15 (Medicine, Dentistry).
2. **Russell Group:** Cardiff University is Wales' only Russell Group member — equivalent to Ivy League prestige in the UK context.
3. **NHS Health Surcharge:** International students must pay the UK Immigration Health Surcharge (currently £776/year) as part of the visa application.
4. **Student Visa:** Apply for a UK Student Visa (replaced Tier 4) via UKVI. Requires Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) from your university.
5. **Work Rights:** International students on a UK Student Visa can work up to 20 hours/week during term and full-time during holidays.
6. **Post-Study Work:** UK Graduate Route allows 2 years' work after Bachelor's/Master's, 3 years after PhD — applies to all UK universities including Wales.
7. **Welsh Language:** Some programs offered in Welsh (Cymraeg) at UWTSD, Aberystwyth, Bangor — English-medium programs available at all universities.
8. **Tuition Fees:** Welsh-domiciled students receive grants and loans from Student Finance Wales. International students pay full international fees with no UK government support.
9. **Foundation Years:** Most Welsh universities offer International Foundation Year programs (1 year) for students who do not meet direct entry requirements.
10. **IELTS Versions Accepted:** IELTS Academic and IELTS UKVI (Academic) accepted for visa purposes. General Training NOT accepted for university admission.
11. **Health Sciences:** Medicine at Cardiff is one of the most competitive in the UK. Requires UCAT or BMAT, strong A-levels (typically AAA including Chemistry), and interview.
12. **Intakes:** Main intake September. Some universities offer January intake for postgraduate programs.
