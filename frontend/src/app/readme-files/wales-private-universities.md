## INSTITUTION PROFILES

### 1. BPP University (Cardiff Campus)
- **Type:** For-profit private university with degree-awarding powers (UK-wide)
- **Founded:** 1975 (as BPP Holdings), university status 2007
- **Focus:** Professional education — Law, Business, Finance, Accounting, Nursing, Psychology
- **Cardiff Presence:** BPP operates programs in Cardiff though its main campuses are in England (London, Manchester, Leeds, Birmingham, Bristol, Nottingham)
- **Key Programs:** LLB Law, BA Business Management, Graduate Diploma in Law (GDL), LPC (Legal Practice Course), MBA, MSc Finance, Nursing (BSc)
- **Accreditations:** Law programs accredited by Solicitors Regulation Authority (SRA) and Bar Standards Board (BSB)
- **Intake:** January and September for most programs
- **Class Sizes:** Small cohorts — typically 15–30 students per class
- **International Students:** Strong reputation for professional law and business qualifications
- **IELTS:** 5.5–6.0 for most programs; 6.5 for some professional programs
- **Special Note:** BPP is one of only a handful of private degree-awarding institutions in the UK with full university title

### 2. Global Banking School (GBS) Cardiff
- **Type:** Private higher education provider
- **Founded:** 2003 (London), Cardiff campus more recent
- **Focus:** Business, Finance, Banking, Accounting, Computing
- **Validation:** Degrees validated by University of West London and Anglia Ruskin University
- **Key Programs:** BA (Hons) Business Management, BA (Hons) Accounting & Finance, BA (Hons) Computing, MBA, MSc International Business
- **Student Profile:** Large proportion of international and mature students
- **IELTS:** 5.5 overall for most programs
- **Location:** Cardiff City Centre
- **Intakes:** Multiple intakes per year — January, May, September
- **Note:** GBS has campuses across England (London, Birmingham, Manchester, Leeds) — Cardiff is one of its newer campuses

### 3. International Academy Wales
- **Type:** Private independent college offering HE-level programs
- **Location:** Swansea (also serves wider South Wales)
- **Focus:** Hospitality, Tourism, Business, Health and Social Care
- **Partnerships:** Some programs validated by Swansea University or Cardiff Metropolitan University
- **Key Programs:** HND/Foundation Degree in Business Management, Tourism & Hospitality, Health & Social Care
- **Entry Level:** Primarily HND and Foundation Degree (Level 4–5), with some full Bachelor's provision
- **IELTS:** 5.5 for HND programs; 6.0 for degree-level programs
- **Student Body:** Mix of local Welsh students and international students (largely from South Asia and Middle East)

### 4. Cardiff and Vale College (CAVC) — HE Division
- **Type:** Further Education College with Higher Education Division
- **Founded:** 2012 (merger of Cardiff and Barry colleges)
- **Location:** Multiple campuses across Cardiff — Cardiff City Centre, Barry, Trowbridge
- **Key HE Programs:** HNC/HND Engineering, HNC/HND Business, Foundation Degrees, Professional certificates
- **Validation:** Programs validated by Cardiff Metropolitan University and University of South Wales
- **IELTS:** 5.5 for most HE programs
- **Student Profile:** Primarily local Welsh students; some international students
- **Tuition (HE programs):** £7,500–£9,500/year for international students
- **Note:** CAVC is one of the largest FE colleges in Wales — excellent for vocational and technical HE pathways

### 5. Coleg Gwent — HE Division
- **Type:** Further Education College with HE provision
- **Location:** Multiple campuses — Newport City, Usk, Pontypool, Ebbw Vale, Crosskeys
- **Key HE Programs:** HNC/HND Engineering, Computing, Business, Health & Social Care
- **Validation:** Programs validated by University of South Wales
- **IELTS:** 5.5 for HE programs
- **Student Profile:** Primarily local Newport/Gwent area students
- **Note:** Most affordable HE route in Wales; strong links to local employers

### 6. NPTC Group of Colleges — HE Division
- **Type:** Further Education College with HE provision
- **Location:** Neath, Port Talbot, Brecon
- **Key HE Programs:** HNC/HND Engineering, Agriculture, Animal Management, Creative Arts
- **Validation:** Programs validated by University of Wales Trinity Saint David
- **IELTS:** 5.5 for HE programs
- **Student Profile:** Primarily local students from Neath/Port Talbot area
- **Note:** Strong in agriculture and engineering programs specific to South Wales industries

## GENERAL NOTES

1. **Limited Private Sector:** Wales has a much smaller private higher education sector than England. Most Welsh students and international students attend publicly-funded institutions.
2. **Validation Model:** Most private colleges in Wales do not have their own degree-awarding powers — they offer programs validated and awarded by recognised public Welsh universities (USW, Cardiff Met, UWTSD).
3. **University of Wales (Validating Body):** The University of Wales validates degrees at institutions worldwide but is not itself a teaching university. Students receive a University of Wales degree but study at the partner college.
4. **FE-HE Pathway:** Wales has a well-developed FE-to-HE pathway system. HNCs and HNDs completed at FE colleges can lead to direct entry into Year 2 or Year 3 of a Bachelor's degree at a public Welsh university.
5. **Student Visa for Private Providers:** BPP University is a licensed Student Visa sponsor. Other private colleges vary — always confirm Student Visa sponsorship status before applying.
6. **Quality Assurance:** All Welsh HE institutions (public and private) are subject to QAA (Quality Assurance Agency) review. Look for QAA-reviewed and approved institutions.
7. **Work Rights:** Same as public universities — 20 hours/week during term, full-time during holidays on Student Visa.
8. **Post-Study Work:** Graduate Route (2 years post-Bachelor's/Master's) applies only to providers with Student Visa sponsorship and degree-awarding powers. Confirm eligibility for the Graduate Route before choosing a private provider.
9. **IELTS UKVI:** For UK Student Visa applications, IELTS UKVI Academic or IELTS Academic (test centre version) required. Online IELTS generally NOT accepted for visa purposes.
10. **Cost-Effectiveness:** For budget-conscious international students, combining an HND at a Welsh FE college with a top-up degree at a public Welsh university can significantly reduce overall costs compared to a full 3-year degree at Cardiff or Swansea.
