## GENERAL NOTES

1. **Group of Eight (Go8):** Australia's 8 leading research universities comparable to the UK Russell Group. All are in the QS Top 100 (Melbourne, Sydney, UNSW at #13–19). Admission is highly competitive.
2. **ATAR System:** The Australian Tertiary Admission Rank is a percentile score from 0–99.95. International students' qualifications are converted to an equivalent ATAR.
3. **Three-Year vs. Four-Year Degrees:** Australian undergraduate degrees are typically 3 years (BA, BSc, B.Com.) with Honours as an optional 4th year. Professional degrees (Medicine, Law, Engineering) are 4–6 years.
4. **Graduate Entry Programs:** Medicine, Law, and some other programs at Melbourne, ANU, and Flinders are graduate-entry only (requiring a prior Bachelor's degree).
5. **Semester System:** Most Australian universities operate on two semesters: Semester 1 (February–June) and Semester 2 (July–November). Trimester system used at some universities (Bond, RMIT, UQ Business).
6. **English Tests Accepted:** IELTS Academic, TOEFL iBT, PTE Academic, Cambridge B2/C1/C2. Always check specific program requirements.
7. **Offshore Study:** Many Australian universities offer offshore programs in partner countries — confirm whether the Australian degree will be issued.
8. **Regional Study Benefits:** Destination Australia scholarship (AUD 15,000/yr) available for students studying at approved regional campuses. Regional study may also benefit Post-Study Work Visa duration.
9. **OSHC:** Overseas Student Health Cover is legally required for all international students on Subclass 500 visa. Cost ~AUD 550–650/year for single student.
10. **Minimum Age for Visa:** Applicants under 18 must have approved welfare arrangements.
11. **Genuine Student Requirement:** Australia's immigration replaced GTE (Genuine Temporary Entrant) test with Genuine Student (GS) test in 2024 — must demonstrate genuine intention to study and comply with visa conditions.
