## Detailed University Profiles

### 1. Yonsei University — QS #62
- **Location:** Seodaemun, Seoul
- **Type:** Private university; Christian (Methodist) foundation (est. 1885 — oldest university in Korea)
- **Colleges:** Liberal Arts, Business, Law (Graduate School), Social Sciences, Sciences, Engineering, Medicine, Dentistry, Nursing, Pharmacy, Human Ecology, Music, Art & Design, Theology, Education, Underwood International College (UIC)
- **Notable:** "Y" in SKY universities; Korea's oldest and globally highest-ranked private university; Yonsei-Korea rivalry ("Yonko Games") is the biggest sporting event in Korea
- **Underwood International College (UIC):**
  - Fully English-taught undergraduate college within Yonsei
  - Liberal arts education modeled on US universities (like a Korean liberal arts college)
  - Programs: Political Science & International Studies, Global Economy, Comparative Literature, Integrated Science & Engineering, Global Leadership
  - IELTS 6.5–7.0 / SAT or equivalent required; highly competitive
- **Graduate programs:** Many English-medium master's and PhD programs; Graduate School of International Studies (GSIS)
- **Medical school:** Severance Hospital — one of Korea's top medical centers (internationally known)
- **Tuition:** KRW 6,000,000–12,000,000/yr
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 2. Korea University (KU) — QS #69
- **Location:** Seongbuk, Seoul
- **Type:** Private university (Chungang Hakwon Foundation); liberal arts and professional focus (est. 1905)
- **Colleges:** Liberal Arts, Science, Business, Law, Engineering, Health Sciences (Medicine, Nursing), International Studies, Political Science & Economics, Education, Computer Science & Cybersecurity
- **Notable:** "K" in SKY universities; traditional rival of Yonsei; Korea's top law and business programs
- **KU International Studies College:**
  - International Studies (English-taught)
  - Korean language programs for international students
- **Business school (KUBS):** AACSB and EQUIS dual-accredited; top MBA in Korea
- **Medical school:** Anam Hospital + Guro Hospital
- **Tuition:** KRW 6,000,000–12,000,000/yr
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 3. Sogang University — QS #501–550
- **Location:** Mapo, Seoul (near Hongik University area)
- **Type:** Jesuit Catholic university (est. 1960); small and elite
- **Departments:** Korean Language & Literature, English Language & Literature, Chinese Culture, History, Philosophy, Religious Studies, Economics, Business Administration, Social Sciences, Computer Science, Electronic Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Chemical Engineering, Life Sciences
- **Notable:** Korea's Jesuit university; known for academic integrity and high educational quality despite smaller size; excellent English language center
- **Language programs:** Sogang's Korean language program (KLEC) is nationally famous — Korean language instruction is the best in Korea according to many
- **English programs:** Growing; strong business and engineering English options
- **Tuition:** KRW 8,000,000–12,000,000/yr (higher than most due to small, elite programs)
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 4. Ewha Womans University — QS #351–400
- **Location:** Seodaemun, Seoul
- **Type:** Women's university (world's largest women's university); est. 1886 by American Methodist missionary
- **Important:** Open to WOMEN ONLY for undergraduate; graduate programs may vary by department
- **Colleges:** Liberal Arts, Social Sciences, Sciences, Engineering, Business, Law, Education, Music, Art & Design, Health Sciences (Medicine, Nursing, Pharmacy), Division of International Studies
- **Notable:** Among the world's top women's universities; graduates hold major positions in Korean government and society; vibrant feminist academic culture
- **International programs:**
  - Division of International Studies — English-taught
  - Graduate School of International Studies (GSIS)
  - Korean Language Institute (KLI)
- **Tuition:** KRW 6,000,000–10,000,000/yr
- **GKS:** Ewha is a designated GKS university
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 5. Sookmyung Women's University
- **Location:** Yongsan, Seoul (near Itaewon)
- **Type:** Women's university (est. 1906)
- **Important:** Open to WOMEN ONLY
- **Departments:** Humanities, Social Sciences, Natural Sciences, Arts, Physical Education, Business Administration, Pharmacy, Family & Consumer Sciences, Engineering, Information Sciences, Human Development
- **Notable:** Strong beauty, fashion, and global service industries connections; near Itaewon (international district)
- **International programs:** Global School program; several English-taught tracks
- **Tuition:** KRW 5,000,000–8,000,000/yr (more affordable women's university option)

### 6. Konkuk University — QS #601–650
- **Location:** Gwangjin, Seoul
- **Type:** Private university; animal and life sciences focus
- **Colleges:** Liberal Arts, Social Sciences, Sciences, Engineering, Architecture, Business, Law, Education, Veterinary Medicine, Agriculture & Animal Bioscience, Bioscience & Biotechnology, Pharmacy, Medicine
- **Notable:** One of Korea's top Veterinary Medicine programs; strong life sciences department
- **Popular among:** Students from Bangladesh, Pakistan, India — notably for veterinary, pharmacy, and biotechnology
- **English programs:** International programs in business and engineering
- **Tuition:** KRW 6,000,000–10,000,000/yr
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 7. Dongguk University
- **Location:** Jung, Seoul (near Myeongdong)
- **Type:** Buddhist-affiliated private university (est. 1906)
- **Colleges:** Buddhist Studies, Humanities, Social Sciences, Sciences, Engineering, Business Administration, Arts (Film, Fine Arts, Music, Drama), Law, Education, Physical Education, Medicine (Ilsan campus)
- **Notable:** Korea's premier film school; notable alumni in Korean cinema (including Oscar winners); Buddhist academic environment
- **Film/Media programs:** Graduate School of Digital Image & Contents; famous in Korean film industry
- **Korean Film School:** Dongguk's film program is the most prestigious in Korea
- **English programs:** Global MBA, some undergraduate English tracks
- **Tuition:** KRW 5,500,000–9,000,000/yr
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 8. Inha University
- **Location:** Nam, Incheon (near Incheon International Airport)
- **Type:** Private research university founded by Korean Air (Hanjin Group); aviation and engineering focus
- **Colleges:** Engineering, Sciences, Business Administration, Social Sciences, Humanities, Education, Law, Medicine, Pharmacy, Arts & Sports
- **Notable:** Leading aerospace/aviation engineering program in Korea; near Incheon Airport; excellent transportation links
- **English programs:** Global Business Administration, some engineering English tracks
- **Aviation engineering:** Top program in Korea; Hanjin/Korean Air industry connections
- **Tuition:** KRW 5,500,000–9,000,000/yr
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 9. Ajou University
- **Location:** Suwon, Gyeonggi Province (30 min from Seoul)
- **Type:** Private research university; medical and engineering focus
- **Colleges:** Humanities, Social Sciences, Natural Sciences, Engineering, Business Administration, Medicine, Pharmacy, Nursing, Software Convergence, International Studies
- **Notable:** Large Ajou University Medical Center; growing international programs; Suwon is home to Samsung headquarters (tech connections)
- **Medical school:** Ajou University Hospital — significant research hospital
- **English programs:** International Studies (fully English); Graduate School of International Studies
- **Tuition:** KRW 5,500,000–9,000,000/yr
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 10. Hankuk University of Foreign Studies (HUFS)
- **Location:** Dongdaemun, Seoul (main); Yongin campus (Gyeonggi)
- **Type:** Private university; world's only university offering 45+ languages
- **Colleges:** English Studies, European Studies, Central Asian Studies, East Asian Studies, Southeast Asian Studies, Middle Eastern Studies, Languages & Diplomacy, Business, Law, Global Studies
- **Notable:** Korea's premier language and international studies university; graduates are top interpreters, translators, diplomats, foreign service officers
- **Languages offered:** English, French, German, Russian, Spanish, Arabic, Hindi, Urdu, Bengali, Turkish, Persian, Japanese, Chinese, Vietnamese, Thai, and 30+ more
- **Bengali/South Asian connection:** HUFS has Bengali and South Asian studies programs; Bangladeshi, Indian students find cultural resonance
- **English programs:** International programs in Global Business and Diplomacy
- **Tuition:** KRW 6,000,000–10,000,000/yr
- **Intakes:** March and September

## GKS (Global Korea Scholarship) — Complete Guide

> **The GKS is the most important scholarship for international students in Korea. It covers essentially ALL costs.**

### What GKS Covers (Graduate Level):
| Benefit | Amount/Description |
|---------|--------------------|
| Tuition | 100% covered (paid directly to university) |
| Monthly Stipend | KRW 900,000 (~USD 680) per month |
| Round-trip Airfare | Economy class; provided on arrival and departure |
| Settlement Allowance | KRW 200,000 (one-time on arrival) |
| Korean Language Training | 1 full year at Korean language institute (pre-degree) |
| Additional Korean Language Allowance | KRW 100,000/mo during language training year |
| Dissertation Research Allowance | KRW 210,000–240,000 (one-time for thesis) |
| Health Insurance | National Health Insurance covered |

### What GKS Covers (Undergraduate Level):
| Benefit | Amount/Description |
|---------|--------------------|
| Tuition | 100% covered |
| Monthly Stipend | KRW 900,000/mo |
| Airfare | Round-trip economy |
| Korean Language Training | 1 year (pre-university) |
| Health Insurance | Covered |

### GKS Application Tracks:
1. **Embassy Track:** Apply through Korean Embassy in your home country; recommended by embassy; quota per country; deadline typically September–November each year; results announced by February
2. **University Track:** Apply directly through a designated GKS partner university; university nominates candidates; more competitive as you compete globally, not just in your country

### GKS Eligibility:
- Citizen of a GKS-eligible country (Bangladesh YES, Pakistan YES, India YES)
- Under 25 for undergraduate (under 40 for graduate)
- GPA equivalent of 2.64/4.0 or higher (minimum B- equivalent)
- Not Korean citizen or permanent resident
- Good health; no serious medical conditions

## Admission Requirements

### General Requirements (All Private Universities)

**For Bachelor's Degree:**
- 12 years of formal education
- IELTS 5.5–7.0 (UIC at Yonsei requires highest; general programs 5.5–6.0)
- TOPIK Level 3–4 for Korean-taught programs
- Academic transcripts; secondary school completion certificate
- SOP / personal statement
- Recommendation letters (2)
- Some programs: SAT scores, portfolio (arts/design), entrance exam

**For Master's Degree:**
- 16-year education (bachelor's degree)
- IELTS 6.0–7.0 / TOPIK Level 4–6
- Research proposal (for research-track)
- Supervisor contact (recommended, especially for science/engineering)
- Letters of recommendation (2–3)
- CV/resume

### Country-Specific Requirements

#### Bangladesh 🇧🇩

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **HSC:** GPA 4.0–5.0 out of 5.0; Yonsei UIC and Korea University require 4.5–5.0
- **SSC:** Also evaluated; both SSC and HSC transcripts required
- **IELTS:** 5.5–6.5; Yonsei UIC requires 6.5–7.0; take at British Council Bangladesh or IDP Dhaka
- **TOPIK:** Recommended for Korean-taught programs; tests available at Korean Cultural Center Dhaka
- **GKS Embassy Track (highly recommended):**
  - Apply through Embassy of the Republic of Korea in Dhaka (located in Gulshan)
  - Typical deadline: September–October each year; check embassy website annually
  - Submit: Application form, GPA cert, transcripts, SOP, recommendation letters, IELTS/TOPIK scores, health statement, personal statement
  - Interview round held at embassy
  - Results announced ~January–February
  - Selected students receive Korean language training for 1 year in Korea before degree program begins
- **Popular universities for BD students:** Yonsei, Korea University, HUFS (Bengali language dept.), Konkuk (veterinary/biotech), Ajou (medicine)
- **Application documents:** SSC + HSC transcripts (attested by Bangladesh Board + Ministry of Education + Ministry of Foreign Affairs), passport, NID copy, SOP, recommendation letters, IELTS score

**Master's Admission:**
- **4-year honours degree** from recognized Bangladesh university (BUET, DU, BRAC, NSU, SUST, etc.)
- **CGPA:** 3.0–3.5 preferred; for Yonsei/Korea University graduate programs, 3.3+ preferred
- **IELTS:** 6.0–6.5 for English programs; TOPIK 4–5 for Korean-medium
- **Research proposal:** Strong, clear research plan aligned with faculty research themes
- **GKS Graduate track:** Embassy nominations plus university track applications both available; apply to both for maximum chances
- **Bank statements:** Required for self-funded applications; show KRW 20,000,000+ equivalent

**Student Visa (D-2):**
- GKS scholars: Visa arranged automatically through NIIED and Embassy
- Self-funded: Certificate of Admission + bank statements + police clearance (Bangladesh Police HQ, attested by MFA)
- Health check: Required; includes TB screening
- Immigration registration within 90 days of arrival in Korea
- Residence card issued by Immigration Office

#### Pakistan 🇵🇰

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **Intermediate/A-levels:** 75–80% for Yonsei/KU; A-levels ABB minimum
- **IELTS:** 5.5–6.5; IDP or British Council Pakistan
- **TOPIK:** Korean Cultural Centers in Karachi, Lahore, Islamabad offer TOPIK test registration
- **GKS:** Apply through Embassy of Korea in Islamabad; Pakistan has annual GKS quota
- **Documents:** Intermediate + Matric transcripts (attested by IBCC + HEC + MFA), IELTS scores, SOP, recommendation letters

**Master's Admission:**
- **16-year BS degree:** HEC-recognized; CGPA 3.0–3.5
- **IELTS:** 6.0–6.5
- **TOPIK:** Level 4+ for Korean-medium programs
- **GKS application:** Strong track record for Pakistani students; engineering and technology fields particularly successful

---

#### India 🇮🇳

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **Class 12:** 75–85% from CBSE/State Boards; 85%+ for Yonsei UIC
- **IELTS:** 5.5–6.5 for English programs; PTE also accepted at some universities
- **TOPIK:** Korean Cultural Centers in Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Hyderabad, Bangalore
- **GKS:** Apply through Embassy of Korea in New Delhi; India has annual quota

**Master's Admission:**
- **Bachelor's degree:** UGC-recognized; 60–70%+ aggregate
- **IELTS:** 6.0–6.5
- **TOPIK:** Level 4+ recommended
- **GRE:** Some English-medium science/engineering programs at Yonsei/KU may accept GRE

## General Notes

- **SKY and prestige:** In Korean society, the SKY universities (SNU, Korea, Yonsei) define academic prestige as strongly as Oxbridge does in the UK; attending SKY significantly affects career trajectory in Korea
- **Women's universities:** Ewha and Sookmyung are open ONLY to female students for undergraduate programs; some graduate programs may admit men — verify with specific department
- **HUFS uniqueness:** HUFS is the ONLY university in the world offering 45 languages; it holds enormous cultural and linguistic value; Bengali, Urdu, Hindi departments make it particularly welcoming for South Asian students
- **Dongguk film school:** If you are interested in Korean cinema (which is globally acclaimed post-Parasite/Minari era), Dongguk University has the most prestigious Korean film program
- **Religious affiliations:** Sogang (Jesuit Catholic), Dongguk (Buddhist), Ewha and Sookmyung (Protestant Christian historically) — all welcome students of all faiths; no religious requirements for admission
- **Work rights:** D-2 Student Visa: 20 hours/week part-time work; full-time during vacation with permission (Part-time employment activity permission card needed from immigration)
- **GKS timing:** Applications are annual; missing the deadline means waiting a full year; check embassy websites each July–August for upcoming cycle
- **Halal food in Seoul:** Available in Itaewon, Mapo (Hongdae), and university cafeterias increasingly offer halal options; Yonsei, KU, HUFS all have halal food options on campus
- **Korean language:** Even for English-taught programs, functional Korean significantly improves life quality; GKS provides free 1-year language training — an excellent bonus
- **Currency note:** 1 KRW ≈ 0.082 BDT | 1 KRW ≈ 0.044 PKR | 1 KRW ≈ 0.055 INR (approximate, April 2026) | GKS monthly stipend KRW 900,000 ≈ BDT 73,800 / PKR 39,600 / INR 49,500
