### C. COUNTRY-SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS

#### Bangladesh

##### Undergraduate

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Accepted Qualification** | Higher Secondary Certificate (HSC) / Alim Certificate |
| **Evaluation** | WES (World Education Services) evaluation required or strongly recommended. Most Ivy League and top privates use WES or NACES-member services |
| **GPA Conversion** | Bangladeshi GPA (out of 5.0): 5.0 = ~4.0 US GPA; 4.5–5.0 = 3.5–3.9; 4.0–4.5 = 3.0–3.5 |
| **English** | HSC English NOT accepted by US universities. Must submit: TOEFL iBT 100–110 (top schools) / IELTS 7.0–7.5 |
| **SAT/ACT** | Required/recommended: SAT 1550+ for Ivies. Test-optional at many schools, but high scores help international applicants from BD |
| **Financial Aid** | Some top schools (Harvard, Yale, Princeton, MIT, Dartmouth) are need-blind for international students → apply for financial aid without fear |
| **CSS Profile** | Required alongside FAFSA waiver for need-based financial aid at most private schools |
| **F-1 Visa** | I-20 form + valid passport + DS-160 + SEVIS fee ($350) + embassy interview |
| **Proof of Funds** | Must demonstrate ability to pay full cost of attendance ($75,000–$90,000/yr) if NOT receiving aid |

##### Master's / PhD

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Accepted Degree** | 4-year Bachelor's Honours degree from a recognized Bangladeshi university (Dhaka, BUET, BRAC, NSU, SUST, etc.) |
| **3-Year Pass Degrees** | Generally NOT accepted at top private research universities without bridging year |
| **WES Evaluation** | Required by most top private graduate programs. Course-by-course evaluation recommended |
| **GPA Minimum** | 3.0/4.0 equivalent (approximately 60%+ aggregate marks or CGPA 3.0/4.0 in BD) |
| **GRE** | Required at most top STEM, Science, Social Science programs. Target: Quant 160+, Verbal 155+, AWA 4.0+ |
| **TOEFL iBT** | 90–110 (program dependent). MIT minimum 100; Harvard/Yale/Princeton typically 100+ |
| **IELTS** | 7.0–7.5 for most top private university graduate programs |
| **Funding (PhD)** | PhD students at top privates (Harvard, MIT, Stanford, Yale) typically receive full tuition waiver + $25,000–$45,000/yr stipend |
| **Funding (MS)** | MS programs at private universities are mostly self-funded unless you receive a fellowship or TA/RA position |

## GENERAL NOTES

1. **Tuition Figures:** All tuition figures shown are for the 2025-2026 academic year. Private university tuition increases by approximately 3–5% annually. The total cost of attendance (tuition + fees + room + board + books + personal expenses) at Ivy League and top private universities is typically $80,000–$95,000/yr.
2. **No In-State/Out-of-State Distinction:** Private universities charge the same tuition regardless of where you are from — US citizen, permanent resident, or international student.
3. **Financial Aid Can Reduce Net Cost Dramatically:** At need-blind schools like Harvard, MIT, Princeton, and Yale, international students from lower-income families may pay $0–$15,000/year total (after receiving $65,000–$80,000 in grants and scholarships). The aid is grants — not loans — at the most generous schools.
4. **SAT/ACT Test-Optional:** Most top private universities became test-optional in 2020. However, MIT has re-reinstated its SAT/ACT requirement. For international applicants, submitting strong scores (SAT 1550+ for Ivies) remains advantageous.
5. **Application Volume is Extreme:** Harvard, Yale, Princeton, Stanford, MIT all receive 50,000–115,000 applications per year for approximately 1,500–2,000 freshman spots. Acceptance rates below 5% mean even near-perfect applicants are frequently rejected.
6. **Holistic Admissions:** Unlike European universities which primarily look at grades and test scores, US top private universities evaluate the "whole student" — academics, essays, recommendations, extracurriculars, character, leadership, and fit with university values.
7. **Currency Conversion:** 1 USD ≈ 110 BDT ≈ 278 PKR ≈ 84 INR (April 2026)
8. **F-1 Student Visa:** Required for all international students. Full-time enrollment mandatory (12+ credit hours UG; 9+ credits grad). OPT: 12 months post-graduation (24-month STEM extension available). SEVIS fee: $350.
9. **CSS Profile:** Available at https://cssprofile.collegeboard.org — submit to each university's Financial Aid office code. Most private universities require CSS Profile for financial aid consideration. International students do NOT file FAFSA (US federal aid) but DO file CSS Profile.
10. **Liberal Arts Colleges:** Do not underestimate LACs like Williams, Amherst, Swarthmore, and Pomona. They have acceptance rates under 15%, offer exceptional undergraduate education, and provide generous financial aid. Many LAC graduates go on to top PhD programs and prestigious careers.
