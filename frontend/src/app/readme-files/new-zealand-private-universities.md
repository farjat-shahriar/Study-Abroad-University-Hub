## Detailed Institution Profiles

### 1. New Zealand College of Chiropractic
- **Location:** Auckland
- **Type:** Specialist private college; only chiropractic college in New Zealand
- **Programs:** Bachelor of Chiropractic Science (BCSc), Doctor of Chiropractic (DC)
- **Duration:** 5-year integrated program (BCSc + DC combined)
- **Accreditation:** Recognized by the New Zealand Chiropractic Board; NZQA accredited
- **International students:** Welcome; small cohort sizes
- **Tuition:** NZD 25,000–30,000/yr
- **Entry requirements:** Strong science background (biology, chemistry); IELTS 6.5
- **Career outcomes:** Registered chiropractor in NZ, Australia, and internationally

### 2. Whitecliffe College of Arts & Design
- **Location:** Auckland (central city campus)
- **Type:** Private degree-granting institution; creative arts focus
- **Programs:**
  - Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA)
  - Bachelor of Design (BDes) — Graphic Design, Fashion Design
  - Master of Fine Arts (MFA) — studio-based research
  - Graduate Diploma in Arts
- **Tuition:** NZD 22,000–28,000/yr (bachelor's); NZD 18,000–22,000 (diploma)
- **Admission:** Portfolio is central to all applications; IELTS 6.0
- **Notable:** Strong industry connections in Auckland's creative sector
- **Intakes:** February and July

### 3. Media Design School
- **Location:** Auckland
- **Type:** Private tertiary institution; specialist creative technology
- **Programs:**
  - Bachelor of Art & Design (Games Design — internationally ranked)
  - Bachelor of Creative Technologies
  - Graduate Diploma in Design
  - Short courses and certificates
- **Rankings:** Consistently ranked among top global game design schools by Princeton Review
- **Tuition:** NZD 22,000–28,000/yr (bachelor's)
- **Admission:** Portfolio of creative work, aptitude test, IELTS 6.0
- **Industry links:** Strong connections with Weta Digital, local studios
- **Intakes:** February (main), July

### 4. Yoobee Colleges
- **Locations:** Auckland, Wellington, Christchurch
- **Type:** Private training establishment; design, IT, business focus
- **Programs:**
  - Diploma in Animation & Motion Design
  - Diploma in Game Design
  - Diploma in Web Design & Development
  - Diploma in Business
  - Certificate programs (Level 4–6)
- **Tuition:** NZD 18,000–25,000/yr (degree-level); NZD 15,000–20,000 (diploma)
- **Admission:** IELTS 5.5–6.0; no portfolio required for most programs
- **Duration:** Most diplomas 1–2 years; degrees 3 years
- **Intakes:** Multiple throughout the year
- **Note:** Offers foundation English and study skills programs

### 5. NorthTec (Northland Institute of Technology)
- **Locations:** Whangarei, Dargaville, Kerikeri, Auckland
- **Type:** Public Institute of Technology (not a university, but distinct from the 8 public universities)
- **Programs:**
  - Engineering, Construction, Trades
  - Business, IT
  - Health and Social Services
  - Creative Arts
- **Tuition:** NZD 18,000–24,000/yr (degree-level); NZD 14,000–20,000 (diploma)
- **Admission:** IELTS 5.5–6.0; specific subject prerequisites for engineering
- **Note:** More vocational/practical focus; bachelor's degree programs available

### 6. Toi Ohomai Institute of Technology
- **Locations:** Rotorua, Tauranga, Taupō
- **Type:** Public Institute of Technology (Bay of Plenty region)
- **Programs:**
  - Tourism and Hospitality Management

- **Notable:** Based in Rotorua — major tourism hub; practical tourism/hospitality programs
- **Admission:** IELTS 5.5–6.0
- **Intakes:** February, July

### 7. Ara Institute of Canterbury
- **Location:** Christchurch (multiple campuses)
- **Type:** Public Institute of Technology (Canterbury/West Coast region)
- **Programs:**
  - Bachelor of Engineering Technology
  - Bachelor of Commerce

  - Certificate programs across many trades

### 8. Te Wānanga o Aotearoa
- **Locations:** Multiple campuses nationwide (Hamilton, Auckland, Rotorua, Wellington, and more)

- **Tuition:** NZD 18,000–22,000/yr (degree); NZD 10,000–18,000 (cert/diploma)
- **Note:** Primarily serves Māori and Pasifika communities; international students welcome for specific programs
- **Unique:** Cultural immersion in Māori learning environment

## Important Context: NZ Private vs. Public

> New Zealand's higher education landscape is dominated by its 8 public universities. The private/polytechnic sector serves important but different roles:

| Characteristic | Public Universities | Private/ITP Sector |
|---------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Research focus | High (research-intensive) | Low to moderate |
| QS Rankings | All ranked globally | Generally unranked |
| Degree programs | Full bachelor's to PhD | Certificate to bachelor's |
| Tuition (international) | NZD 24,000–45,000/yr | NZD 15,000–30,000/yr |
| Class sizes | Larger | Smaller, more personal |
| Industry pathways | Academic + industry | Primarily industry-focused |
| International reputation | Strong (especially Auckland, Otago) | Niche/specialist |

## Admission Requirements

### General Requirements (All Private Institutions)

**For Bachelor's Programs:**
- Completed secondary school education (NCEA Level 3 equivalent)
- IELTS Academic: 5.5–6.5 (varies by program; creative arts typically 6.0)
- Portfolio or aptitude test (for design/creative programs)
- Minimum age typically 18; mature entry (20+) considered without formal qualifications

**For Diploma/Certificate Programs:**
- Secondary school completion or equivalent
- IELTS: 5.5 general minimum
- No prior qualifications needed for Level 4–5 certificates
- Interview may be required for specialist programs

### Country-Specific Requirements

#### Bangladesh 🇧🇩

**For Bachelor's Programs:**
- **HSC:** GPA 3.0–4.0 out of 5.0 (lower than public universities)
- **IELTS:** 5.5–6.0 (taken at British Council Bangladesh or IDP Dhaka)
- **Portfolio:** Required for design/creative programs (Whitecliffe, Media Design School, Yoobee)
- **SOP and recommendation letters:** Generally required
- **Application:** Direct application to institution; no common application portal
- **Foundation/pathway:** Many institutions offer English language courses as pathway for students with lower IELTS

**Visa Requirements:**
- Student visa through Immigration New Zealand
- Proof of funds: NZD 15,000/yr living + full tuition
- Police clearance from Bangladesh Police HQ or district police
- Medical certificate from approved physician
- Genuine intention to study (GTE assessment)
- NZQA-accredited institution required for student visa eligibility

---

#### Pakistan 🇵🇰

**For Bachelor's Programs:**
- **FSc / A-levels:** 60–70% or BBC equivalent for FSc; A-levels for direct entry
- **IELTS:** 5.5–6.0 (IDP or British Council Pakistan)
- **Portfolio:** Required for creative programs
- **HEC recognition:** Diplomas from Pakistani institutions may not directly transfer; check with individual institution

**Visa Requirements:**
- Same as general NZ student visa process
- Proof of financial support (bank statements, sponsor letter)
- No-objection certificate from employer (if currently employed)
- Police character certificate

---

#### India 🇮🇳

**For Bachelor's Programs:**
- **Class 12:** 60–70% aggregate (lower bar than public universities)
- **IELTS:** 5.5–6.0 (widely available across India)
- **Portfolio:** Required for design/creative programs
- **PTE/TOEFL:** Also accepted at most institutions

**Visa Requirements:**
- Online student visa application via Immigration NZ
- Biometrics at VFS Global India
- Proof of funds and accommodation
- Insurance documentation

## General Notes

- **NZQA Accreditation:** All institutions must be registered with the New Zealand Qualifications Authority (NZQA) to enroll international students; always verify accreditation status
- **EER Ratings:** NZQA provides External Evaluation and Review ratings for private providers; check ratings before enrolling
- **Work rights:** Same as public universities — 20 hrs/week during study, full-time during breaks (Student Visa holders)
- **Post-study work visa:** Available after completion of level 7 (bachelor's) or above qualifications; some ITP graduates may qualify
- **Language pathways:** Most institutions offer ELICOS (English Language Intensive Courses for Overseas Students) as a pathway to academic programs
- **Accommodation:** Smaller institutions typically do not provide student hostels; students arrange private accommodation
- **Currency note:** 1 NZD ≈ 66 BDT | 1 NZD ≈ 35 PKR | 1 NZD ≈ 50 INR (approximate, April 2026)
- **Recognition:** Degrees from New Zealand College of Chiropractic, Whitecliffe, and Media Design School are NZQA-recognized; always verify recognition in home country
- **Recommendation:** For internationally recognized degrees and better employment outcomes, the 8 public universities remain the preferred choice for international students from Bangladesh, Pakistan, and India
