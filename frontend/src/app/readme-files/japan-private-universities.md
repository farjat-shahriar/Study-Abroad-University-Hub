## Detailed University Profiles

### 1. Keio University — QS #189
- **Location:** Mita, Minato (main campus); Hiyoshi, Yokohama; Shonan Fujisawa campus
- **Type:** Japan's oldest modern university (est. 1858 by Fukuzawa Yukichi)
- **Faculties:** Letters, Economics, Law, Business and Commerce, Medicine, Science and Technology, Pharmacy, Nursing and Medical Care, Policy Management, Environment and Information Studies
- **Notable:** Japan's most prestigious private university alongside Waseda; famous alumni include Japan's leading businesspeople and politicians
- **English programs:**
  - GIGA (Global Information and Governance Academics) at SFC
  - Several graduate-level English programs (law, economics, media)
  - Keio-Princeton dual degree program
- **Medical school:** Highly competitive; Japanese required; considered top-tier in Japan
- **Tuition:** JPY 800,000–1,800,000/yr depending on faculty
- **Intakes:** April and September (some programs)

### 2. Waseda University — QS #200
- **Location:** Nishi-Waseda, Shinjuku, Tokyo (multiple campuses in Tokyo)
- **Type:** Comprehensive private research university (est. 1882)
- **Faculties/Schools:** Political Science & Economics, Law, Commerce, Education, Science & Engineering, Humanities & Social Sciences, Culture, Media & Society, International Liberal Studies, Advanced Science & Engineering, Creative Science & Engineering
- **Notable:** Japan's most international-friendly private university; significant student political activism tradition
- **English programs:**
  - SILS (School of International Liberal Studies) — fully English-taught undergraduate
  - TAISI (Trans-Atlantic and Trans-Pacific Student Exchange Program)
  - Many graduate schools have English-track programs
  - Global Japanese Studies program
- **Tuition:** JPY 800,000–1,800,000/yr (SILS: ~JPY 1,200,000/yr)
- **International students:** ~9,000 international students; large community
- **Intakes:** April and September

### 3. Sophia University (Jochi Daigaku) — QS #401–450
- **Location:** Yotsuya, Chiyoda, Tokyo
- **Type:** Jesuit Catholic university (est. 1913); known for international/multicultural campus
- **Faculties:** Theology, Humanities, Human Sciences, Law, Global Studies, Economics, Science and Technology, Liberal Arts (English Division), Foreign Languages
- **Notable:** Sophia's Faculty of Liberal Arts (FLA) is Japan's oldest English-medium undergraduate program; known for multicultural, international atmosphere
- **English programs:**
  - Faculty of Liberal Arts (FLA) — FULLY English-taught undergraduate (degree in Liberal Arts)
  - Faculty of Global Studies — bilingual English/Japanese
  - Several graduate programs in English
- **Tuition:** JPY 700,000–1,500,000/yr (FLA: ~JPY 1,300,000/yr)
- **Campus culture:** Catholic values; international student community; near central Tokyo
- **Intakes:** April and September

### 4. Ritsumeikan University — QS #601–650

- **Faculties:** Law, Economics, Business Administration, International Relations, Policy Science, Letters, Sciences, Engineering, Pharmacy, Information Science, Mapping Sciences, Sports & Health Sciences, Image Arts & Sciences
- **Notable:** Large university with strong international relations programs; Kyoto location; strong activist/progressive tradition
- **English programs:** College of International Relations (English track); multiple graduate programs
- **Tuition:** JPY 750,000–1,600,000/yr
- **Intakes:** April and September

### 5. Doshisha University — QS #601–650

## Admission Requirements

### General Requirements (All Private Universities)

**For Bachelor's Degree:**
- 12 years of formal education
- For English-taught undergraduate programs: IELTS 6.0–7.0 (SILS/Waseda requires 6.5+); TOEFL iBT 80–100
- University-specific entrance examinations (academic content tests)
- Transcripts, recommendation letters, personal statement
- Some programs: portfolio, interview
- EJU may or may not be required (check individual university)

**For Master's Degree:**
- Bachelor's degree or equivalent 16-year education
- IELTS 6.0–7.0 depending on university and program
- Research proposal (graduate programs)
- Supervisor agreement (especially for research degrees)
- Written entrance exam + interview
- Letters of recommendation (2–3)

### Country-Specific Requirements

#### Bangladesh 🇧🇩

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **HSC:** GPA 4.5–5.0 for competitive programs (Keio, Waseda, Sophia)
- **English programs recommended:** SILS at Waseda, FLA at Sophia, Global Studies at Ritsumeikan offer full English-medium undergraduate degrees
- **IELTS:** 6.0–7.0; Waseda SILS typically requires 6.5+; take at British Council Bangladesh or IDP Dhaka
- **Application steps:**
  1. Prepare academic transcripts (SSC + HSC); get attested by Bangladesh Education Board
  2. Take IELTS and/or TOEFL
  3. Write personal statement / study plan
  4. Take JLPT (if applying to Japanese-medium programs)
  5. Apply online through each university's admissions portal
  6. Receive offer → Apply for student visa (COE from Japanese Immigration)
- **MEXT Scholarship path (recommended):** Apply through Embassy of Japan in Dhaka (May–June each year); screening includes written exam, interview; results ~December; MEXT covers all costs
- **Private university-specific entrance exams:** Most English-track undergrad programs require an academic ability test (math, essay, language) in addition to IELTS

**Master's Admission:**
- **Bachelor's degree:** 4-year honours from recognized Bangladesh university
- **CGPA:** 3.0–3.5 preferred
- **Research plan:** Detailed, well-researched document aligned with professor's current research
- **Supervisor contact:** Email professors with your research plan BEFORE submitting formal application; get informal agreement first
- **IELTS:** 6.5–7.0 for English graduate programs at Keio, Waseda, Sophia
- **Funded pathway:** MEXT (university recommended) or Rotary Peace Fellowship (for specific programs)

**Student Visa:**
- Certificate of Eligibility (COE) issued by Japanese Immigration and submitted by university
- Apply for student visa at Embassy of Japan in Dhaka presenting COE
- Processing: 1–4 weeks after COE received
- Required documents: Application form, photo, passport, COE, financial evidence
- Self-funded proof: Bank statements showing equivalent of JPY 2,000,000+

#### Pakistan 🇵🇰

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **Intermediate/FSc/A-levels:** Top grades required; A-levels ABB or better for Keio/Waseda
- **IELTS:** 6.0–6.5 for most programs; 6.5–7.0 for Waseda SILS and Keio
- **MEXT:** Applications through Embassy of Japan Islamabad; competitive; strong science/math background valued
- **English tests:** TOEFL also widely accepted; check individual university requirements
- **Foundation/prep:** Japanese language study helps even for English programs; consider Japan Foundation courses

**Master's Admission:**
- **16-year education** (4-year BS)
- **CGPA:** 3.0–3.5 minimum
- **Supervisor contact:** Critical step; Japanese professors value formal, polite, well-researched initial emails
- **IELTS:** 6.5–7.0

#### India 🇮🇳

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **Class 12:** 80%+ from CBSE/State Boards for competitive universities
- **IELTS:** 6.0–6.5; PTE also accepted at some institutions
- **MEXT:** Applications through Embassy of Japan in Delhi/Mumbai/Chennai/Kolkata/Bangalore
- **SAT/academic tests:** Some English-medium programs may reference SAT scores

**Master's Admission:**
- **Bachelor's degree:** UGC-recognized; minimum 60%
- **CGPA:** 3.0–3.5
- **IELTS/TOEFL:** Required for all English-medium programs
- **GRE:** Some science/engineering programs at private universities may welcome GRE scores

## General Notes

- **MARCH universities:** Meiji, Aoyama, Rikkyo, Chuo, Hosei — collectively known as MARCH in Japan; highly respected private Tokyo universities below Waseda/Keio tier
- **Waseda and Keio:** Considered equivalent in prestige to top national universities except Tokyo/Kyoto; graduates are widely employed by Japan's top companies
- **English programs:** Private universities lead in English-taught undergraduate programs (SILS at Waseda, FLA at Sophia are models for English immersion in Japan)
- **Work rights:** Part-time work up to 28 hours/week permitted with student visa; permission stamp required
- **Japanese language:** Even in English programs, learning Japanese significantly improves quality of life and employment prospects in Japan; all universities offer free or subsidized Japanese language classes for international students
- **Post-graduation:** Japan's April hiring season ("shushoku katsudo") is unique; international graduates can participate; Japanese language essential for most jobs
- **MEXT priority:** If choosing between public and private, MEXT scholarship can fund study at ANY Japanese national or private university; getting MEXT removes financial concern entirely
- **Academic calendar:** April is the main start for all programs; October intake increasingly offered especially for international/English programs
