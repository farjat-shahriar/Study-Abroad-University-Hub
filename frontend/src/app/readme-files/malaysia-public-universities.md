## Detailed University Profiles

### 1. Universiti Malaya (UM) — QS #65
- **Location:** Kuala Lumpur (Pantai Dalam campus)
- **Type:** Flagship public research university; Malaysia's oldest (est. 1949)
- **Faculties:** Medicine, Engineering, Computer Science & IT, Business, Law, Arts, Science, Built Environment
- **Notable:** Only Malaysian university consistently in QS Top 100
- **Medical program (MBBS):** Highly competitive; MYR 40,000–60,000/yr for international students
- **Undergraduate tuition:** MYR 15,000–40,000/yr
- **Postgraduate tuition:** MYR 18,000–45,000/yr
- **Language of instruction:** English and Bahasa Malaysia (varies by program)
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 2. Universiti Sains Malaysia (USM) — QS #133
- **Location:** Main campus in Penang; engineering campus in Nibong Tebal; health campus in Kubang Kerian, Kelantan
- **Strengths:** Health Sciences, Engineering, Sciences, Social Sciences
- **Notable:** Malaysia's first APEX (Accelerated Programme for Excellence) university; strong research output
- **Medical school:** School of Medical Sciences (Kubang Kerian campus)
- **Undergraduate tuition:** MYR 12,000–38,000/yr
- **Postgraduate tuition:** MYR 15,000–40,000/yr
- **Intakes:** September (main)

### 3. Universiti Putra Malaysia (UPM) — QS #129
- **Location:** Serdang, Selangor (near Kuala Lumpur)
- **Strengths:** Agriculture, Veterinary Science, Food Technology, Engineering, Sciences
- **Notable:** Malaysia's leading agricultural university; also strong in engineering and medicine
- **Medical school:** Faculty of Medicine and Health Sciences
- **Undergraduate tuition:** MYR 12,000–35,000/yr
- **Postgraduate tuition:** MYR 15,000–38,000/yr
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 4. Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia (UKM) — QS #141
- **Location:** Bangi, Selangor
- **Strengths:** Medical Sciences, Engineering, Social Sciences, Malay Studies
- **Notable:** Strong Malay language programs; growing English-medium offerings
- **Medical school:** Faculty of Medicine at Cheras campus
- **Undergraduate tuition:** MYR 12,000–35,000/yr
- **Postgraduate tuition:** MYR 14,000–38,000/yr
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 5. Universiti Teknologi Malaysia (UTM) — QS #155
- **Location:** Main campus Johor Bahru; UTM KL campus in Kuala Lumpur
- **Strengths:** Engineering, Architecture, Built Environment, Technology
- **Notable:** Malaysia's premier technology university; strong in civil/electrical/mechanical engineering
- **Undergraduate tuition:** MYR 13,000–35,000/yr
- **Postgraduate tuition:** MYR 15,000–38,000/yr
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 6. Universiti Teknologi MARA (UiTM) — IMPORTANT RESTRICTION
- **Location:** Shah Alam (main); 30+ campuses nationwide
- **CRITICAL NOTE:** UiTM enrollment is **restricted to Bumiputera (Malay and indigenous) Malaysians only** under Malaysian law. **International students including Bangladeshis, Pakistanis, and Indians CANNOT enroll in UiTM.**
- **Size:** Largest university in Malaysia by enrollment
- **Programs offered:** Business, Engineering, IT, Sciences, Health, Law, Art & Design

### 7. Universiti Utara Malaysia (UUM) — QS #601–650
- **Location:** Sintok, Kedah (northern Malaysia)
- **Strengths:** Business, Management, IT, Public Administration
- **Notable:** Malaysia's dedicated management university
- **Undergraduate tuition:** MYR 10,000–25,000/yr
- **Postgraduate tuition:** MYR 12,000–28,000/yr
- **Intakes:** March and September

### 8. Universiti Islam Antarabangsa Malaysia (IIUM) — QS #451–500
- **Location:** Gombak, Selangor (main); Kuantan campus (medicine)
- **Strengths:** Islamic Studies, Law (Islamic jurisprudence), Medicine, Engineering, Economics
- **Notable:** International Islamic University with global Muslim student community; large Bangladeshi student population
- **Language:** English (medium of instruction for most programs) + Arabic (Islamic disciplines)
- **Undergraduate tuition:** MYR 10,000–28,000/yr
- **Postgraduate tuition:** MYR 12,000–30,000/yr
- **Special appeal:** Culturally comfortable for Muslim students from Bangladesh, Pakistan

## Admission Requirements

### General Requirements (All Public Universities)

**For Bachelor's Degree:**
- Completed secondary/pre-university education (equivalent to SPM + STPM/Foundation or A-levels/equivalent)
- IELTS Academic: 5.5–6.5 (varies by university and program)
- TOEFL iBT: 75–90+ (program-dependent)
- Muet (Malaysian University English Test) Band 3–5 accepted for some programs
- Medical programs: Higher language requirements (IELTS 6.5–7.0)
- Mathematics and sciences required for STEM programs

**For Master's Degree:**
- Recognized bachelor's degree (3-year minimum; 4-year honours preferred)
- CGPA minimum: 2.75 out of 4.0 (some programs require 3.0+)
- IELTS: 6.0–6.5 (postgraduate level)
- Research master's: supervisor agreement and research proposal required
- Professional master's: may require work experience

### Country-Specific Requirements

#### Bangladesh 🇧🇩

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **HSC (Higher Secondary Certificate):** GPA 4.0–5.0 out of 5.0; top universities (UM, USM, UPM, UKM, UTM) prefer GPA 5.0
- **SSC + HSC:** Both evaluated; science stream preferred for STEM programs
- **O-level / A-level equivalency:** O-levels with at least 5Cs and A-levels with minimum 2Cs (BBC equivalent) accepted
- **IELTS:** 5.5–6.5 (British Council Bangladesh or IDP Dhaka); Medical programs require 6.5–7.0
- **NUFFIC/MQA recognition:** Check if your Bangladesh board results are recognized — generally HSC from all boards accepted
- **MBBS International Program:** Highly competitive at UM and UPM; IELTS 6.5+ required; separate medical entrance may apply
- **Application documents:** HSC and SSC transcripts (original + certified copies), NID/passport copy, SOP, recommendation letters, passport photos
- **IIUM:** Particularly welcoming to Bangladeshi Muslim students; strong BD student community

**Master's Admission:**
- **Bachelor's degree:** 4-year honours from UGC-recognized Bangladesh university (BUET, DU, BRAC, AIUB, NSU etc.)
- **CGPA:** Minimum 2.75 out of 4.0 (strong research universities prefer 3.0+)
- **IELTS:** 6.0–6.5
- **Scholarship applications:** Strongly recommended — Malaysian government scholarships and university fellowships available
- **Processing time:** 3–6 months for complete applications

**Malaysian Student Pass for Bangladeshi Students:**
- EMGS (Education Malaysia Global Services) processes student pass applications
- University submits application on behalf of student
- Visa Approval Letter (VAL) issued before travel
- Health screening on arrival at approved clinic
- Documents: Offer letter, passport, photos, financial proof, medical report from Bangladesh (pre-departure health check)
- Processing time: 4–8 weeks
- Cost: ~MYR 1,800–2,500 total for student pass processing

---

#### Pakistan 🇵🇰

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **Intermediate / A-levels / IB:** Minimum 65–70% in Intermediate; A-levels BBC or better
- **IELTS:** 5.5–6.5 (IDP or British Council Pakistan)
- **HEC Equivalency:** May be required for recognition of previous qualifications
- **Medical programs:** IELTS 6.5+; some universities require MDCAT equivalent or may require direct interview
- **IIUM:** Popular among Pakistani students due to Islamic environment and affordable costs
- **Documents:** Intermediate + Matric transcripts, school leaving certificate, character certificate, SOP

**Master's Admission:**
- **16-year education:** Standard requirement — 4-year BS or equivalent
- **2-year BSc:** May not be sufficient for direct master's entry; may need to complete a bridge program
- **CGPA:** 2.75–3.0 out of 4.0
- **IELTS:** 6.0–6.5
- **Supervisor contact:** For research programs, contacting a faculty supervisor before applying is strongly recommended

**Student Pass for Pakistani Students:**
- Same EMGS process as Bangladeshi students
- Police character certificate from Pakistan (NADRA-attested)
- Financial proof: Bank statements showing MYR 30,000+ per year

---

#### India 🇮🇳

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **Class 12 / 10+2:** Minimum 65–75% aggregate from CBSE, ICSE, or State Boards
- **IELTS:** 5.5–6.5; PTE Academic also accepted
- **NEET (for medicine):** Not required by Malaysian universities, but competitive MBBS programs are limited for Indian students
- **Documents:** Class 10 and 12 mark sheets, school leaving certificate, SOP, recommendation letters

**Master's Admission:**
- **Bachelor's degree:** UGC-recognized 3-year or 4-year degree from Indian university
- **CGPA:** 3.0/4.0 or 60%+ marks
- **IELTS:** 6.0–6.5
- **GMAT/GRE:** Not typically required but strengthens MBA applications at UM and UUM

**Student Pass for Indian Students:**
- EMGS online application; biometrics may be required
- VFS Global India handles some Malaysia visa documentation
- Proof of funds and accommodation details required

## General Notes

- **Language of instruction:** Most top public universities now offer programs fully in English; some programs still require Bahasa Malaysia proficiency
- **Student Pass (not Visa):** Malaysia uses a Student Pass system administered by the Immigration Department, processed through EMGS; different from a traditional student visa
- **EMGS:** Education Malaysia Global Services is the mandatory gateway for international student pass applications — all universities submit through EMGS
- **Health screening:** Medical examination required on arrival at approved AKHMS clinic; includes chest X-ray, blood tests
- **Work rights:** International students in Malaysia generally cannot work part-time during studies (unlike NZ, Australia, UK); check current regulations
- **Muslim-friendly:** Malaysia is a Muslim-majority country; halal food widely available; prayer facilities on all campuses; culturally comfortable for Muslim students from Bangladesh and Pakistan
- **Cost advantage:** Malaysia offers significantly lower tuition and living costs compared to Western destinations while maintaining globally ranked universities
- **Medical/Dental programs:** International MBBS at Malaysian public universities is available but competitive and expensive (MYR 40,000–60,000/yr); many Bangladeshi students pursue MBBS here
- **MOHE recognition:** Degrees from all listed universities are recognized by Malaysia's Ministry of Higher Education; check recognition in home country before enrolling
- **Academic calendar:** September intake is main; March intake available at most universities
- **UiTM restriction:** Universiti Teknologi MARA (UiTM) is legally restricted to Bumiputera Malaysians only. International students from ANY country cannot enroll. This is not a policy preference but a statutory requirement under Malaysian law.
