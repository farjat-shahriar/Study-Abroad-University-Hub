## Admission Requirements

### General Requirements (All Private Universities)

**For Bachelor's Degree:**
- O-level / SPM / IGCSE with minimum 5 credits (B or above in relevant subjects)
- Pre-university qualifications: A-levels (2 subjects minimum), STPM, Foundation (1 year), Diploma, IB
- IELTS Academic: 5.5–6.5 (branch campuses: 6.0–6.5; Malaysian private: 5.5–6.0)
- TOEFL iBT: 75–90 (varies by institution)
- PTE Academic: 45–58

**For Master's Degree:**
- Relevant bachelor's degree (minimum 2nd class honours or CGPA 2.5+)
- IELTS: 6.0–6.5
- Some programs require professional qualifications (CPA, CFA for finance)
- Research programs: research proposal and supervisor agreement

### Country-Specific Requirements

#### Bangladesh 🇧🇩

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **HSC:** GPA 4.0–5.0; top branch campuses (Monash, Nottingham, Southampton) prefer strong grades + English proficiency
- **O-level/A-level (British curriculum):** Highly valued at branch campuses; BBC or better for Nottingham/Southampton
- **IELTS:** 5.5–6.5; branch campuses require 6.0–6.5; test taken at British Council Bangladesh or IDP Dhaka
- **Foundation programs:** Many universities offer 1-year foundation as an alternative pathway — widely used by Bangladeshi students who do not meet direct entry requirements
- **SOP + recommendations:** Generally required for applications
- **BRAC, NSU, AIUB students:** Credit transfer arrangements exist with some Malaysian private universities
- **Popular choices for BD students:** Monash Malaysia (engineering/business), Nottingham Malaysia, IIUM (already listed in public), APU (IT)

**Master's Admission:**
- **4-year honours degree from Bangladesh:** Fully accepted; CGPA 2.75–3.0 minimum
- **IELTS:** 6.0–6.5
- **Twinning/double degree programs:** Available at several private universities with UK counterparts
- **Scholarship applications:** Submit with main application; funding available from Malaysian government (MIS) and individual universities

**Malaysian Student Pass for Bangladeshi Students:**
- **EMGS (Education Malaysia Global Services):** Mandatory processing agency
  - Step 1: Obtain offer letter from university
  - Step 2: University submits Student Pass application to EMGS on your behalf
  - Step 3: Pay EMGS processing fee (~MYR 1,800–2,500)
  - Step 4: Receive Visa Approval Letter (VAL) — takes 4–8 weeks
  - Step 5: Travel to Malaysia and complete health screening (AKHMS clinic)
  - Step 6: Collect Student Pass (valid for 1 year; renewable annually)
- **Health screening:** Chest X-ray, blood tests, urine test — must be done at approved clinic in Malaysia
- **Documents needed:** Offer letter, HSC/SSC transcripts (attested), passport (min 18 months validity), photos, NID copy, financial proof, medical certificate from Bangladesh physician (pre-departure)
- **Living costs:** Budget MYR 1,000–1,800/month in KL; less in smaller cities

#### Pakistan 🇵🇰

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **Intermediate / FSc:** 65–75% for standard private universities; 75–80% for branch campuses
- **A-levels:** BBC minimum for branch campuses; B/C grades for other private universities
- **IELTS:** 5.5–6.5 (IDP or British Council Pakistan)
- **Foundation programs:** Available as alternative entry pathway at most institutions
- **IBCC (Inter Board Committee of Chairmen):** Equivalency certificate may be required for recognition

**Master's Admission:**
- **16-year education:** 4-year BS degree or equivalent
- **CGPA:** 2.75–3.0 minimum
- **IELTS:** 6.0–6.5
- **HEC recognition of Malaysian degree:** Check MQA (Malaysian Qualifications Agency) register before enrolling to ensure degree is HEC-recognized for Pakistan

**Student Pass:**
- Same EMGS process as BD students
- Pakistani police clearance (NADRA-issued, apostilled/attested)
- Financial sponsorship letter if sponsored by family

#### India 🇮🇳

**Bachelor's Admission:**
- **Class 12 / 10+2:** 65–75% from CBSE, State Boards, or ICSE; branch campuses require 75%+
- **IELTS:** 5.5–6.5; also accept PTE Academic
- **Foundation:** Available for students not meeting direct entry criteria

**Master's Admission:**
- **Bachelor's degree:** UGC-recognized; 3 or 4 years accepted
- **CGPA:** 3.0/4.0 or 60%+ marks
- **IELTS:** 6.0–6.5

**Student Pass:**
- EMGS process; biometrics collected in Malaysia on arrival
- Indian students: fastest-growing segment in Malaysian private universities
- Many institutions have dedicated India student support offices

## General Notes

- **Branch campus advantage:** Degrees from Monash, Nottingham, and Southampton Malaysia are awarded by the parent university in Australia/UK — globally recognized and often preferred by employers
- **MQA registration:** All private universities must be registered with Malaysian Qualifications Agency (MQA); always verify before enrolling; check mqa.gov.my
- **EMGS mandatory:** Education Malaysia Global Services processes ALL international student passes; applications submitted by the university, not the student directly
- **Work restrictions:** International students in Malaysia generally may not engage in part-time employment during studies; check current MyEG/Immigration policies
- **Halal food:** Widely available throughout Malaysia; Muslim-friendly environment across all campuses
- **Language:** English is primary language of instruction at all listed private universities; no Bahasa Malaysia requirement for international students at private institutions
- **Dual degree programs:** Several private universities offer twinning (1+2 or 2+1) with UK/Australian universities at significantly lower cost than studying fully abroad
- **Credit transfer:** Some Malaysian private universities have credit transfer agreements with institutions in Bangladesh, Pakistan, and India — verify with your current institution
- **Currency note:** 1 MYR ≈ 28 BDT | 1 MYR ≈ 15 PKR | 1 MYR ≈ 18 INR (approximate, April 2026)
- **MDAB (Malaysian Degree Assessment Board):** Medical degrees from private universities must be recognized by the Malaysian Medical Council (MMC) and verified in home country before practicing
- **Intakes:** Most private universities offer multiple intakes per year (January/February, March/April, July/August, September/October) — more flexibility than public universities
