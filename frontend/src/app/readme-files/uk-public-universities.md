## GENERAL NOTES

1. **Brexit Impact**: Since 2021, EU/EEA students are treated as international students for fee purposes — no longer eligible for home fees or UK student loans.
2. **UCAS**: All undergraduate applications in the UK go through UCAS (ucas.com). Students can apply to maximum 5 courses. Medicine applicants limited to 4 medical choices.
3. **Oxbridge**: Oxford and Cambridge have separate application processes — must submit UCAS application before October 15 deadline (vs January 31 for other universities).
4. **Russell Group**: 24 research-intensive universities — most prestigious in UK higher education. Employers and international graduate schools give significant weight to Russell Group degrees.
5. **Post-Study Work Visa (Graduate Route)**: Non-EU international students can stay 2 years after graduation (3 years for PhD graduates) to seek employment — applicable to BD, PK, IN students.
6. **NHS Surcharge**: Mandatory for all international students on Student Visa — £776/yr (2026 rate) paid upfront with visa application. Gives full access to National Health Service.
7. **ATAS Certificate**: Required for students studying certain advanced science, engineering, or technology subjects — apply via ATAS portal.
8. **Foundation Year / International Year One**: Students who don't meet direct entry requirements can take a 1-year foundation program (also known as International Year One) at most UK universities.
9. **Scottish Universities**: Home Scottish students get free tuition at Scottish universities (SAAS funded). International students pay full fees. Note: Scottish Bachelor's degrees are typically 4 years (compared to 3 years in England).
10. **Joint Honors / Combined Degrees**: UK universities commonly offer joint honors degrees (e.g., Economics and Politics; Computer Science and Mathematics) — more flexible curriculum than many countries.
