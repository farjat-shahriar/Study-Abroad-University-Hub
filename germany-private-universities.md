## KEY DIFFERENCES: PUBLIC vs PRIVATE UNIVERSITIES IN GERMANY

| Feature | Public Universities | Private Universities |
|---------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Tuition Fees** | Free (except BW: €1,500/sem for non-EU) | €5,000 – €30,000+ per year |
| **Semester Fee** | €100 – €400 / semester | Usually included in tuition |
| **Class Sizes** | Large (100-500+ students per lecture) | Small (20-50 students) |
| **Total Students** | ~2.5 million (87% of all students) | ~373,000 (13% of all students) |
| **Number of Institutions** | ~281 | ~141 |
| **Admission** | NC (Numerus Clausus) based | Often own selection process |
| **Teaching Language** | Mostly German, growing English options | More English-taught programs |
| **Industry Connections** | Good but varies | Often stronger / built-in internships |
| **Research Focus** | Strong research orientation | More practice-oriented |
| **Recognition** | Fully recognized | State-recognized (staatlich anerkannt) |

---

## KEY NOTES

1. **State Recognition**: All private universities must be "staatlich anerkannt" (state-recognized) to award valid degrees. All institutions in this list are recognized.
2. **Largest Private University**: IU International University of Applied Sciences (IU) is by far the largest with 130,000+ students across 30+ campuses and online.
3. **Church Universities**: Kirchliche Hochschulen are open to students of all faiths. They are particularly strong in social work, education, and nursing programs.
4. **FOM University**: FOM is the largest private on-campus university with 57,000+ students, offering evening/weekend programs for working professionals.
5. **SRH Network**: SRH operates multiple universities across Germany (Berlin, Heidelberg, Hamm, Riedlingen, etc.).
6. **Hochschule Fresenius Network**: Fresenius operates campuses in Idstein, Heidelberg, Hamburg, Cologne, Munich, Düsseldorf, Berlin, Frankfurt, and Wiesbaden.
7. **Accreditation**: All programs must be accredited by German accreditation agencies (e.g., ACQUIN, AQAS, FIBAA, ZEvA).
