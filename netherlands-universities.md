## TUITION FEE STRUCTURE — NETHERLANDS OVERVIEW (2025-2026)

### Statutory Fee vs Institutional Fee

The Netherlands has a **dual fee system** set by Dutch law:

| Fee Type | Who Pays | Amount 2025-2026 | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------------------|-------|
| **Statutory Fee** | EU/EEA/Swiss/Surinamese nationals | **€2,601/yr** | Set by Dutch government, same for ALL programmes |
| **Institutional Fee** | Non-EU/EEA students | **€8,000–€32,000/yr** | Set by each university, varies by programme |
| **Halved Statutory Fee** | First-year EU/EEA bachelor students | **€1,301/yr** | 50% discount in year 1 only |

> **Important:** The statutory fee of €2,601 applies ONLY if: (1) You hold EU/EEA/Swiss/Surinamese nationality, AND (2) You are enrolled in your FIRST government-funded bachelor's or master's programme. If you already hold a Dutch-funded degree and enrol in a second one, you pay the institutional fee even as an EU citizen.

## COUNTRY-SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS

### Bangladesh 🇧🇩

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **HSC Diploma Equivalence** | Nuffic rates Bangladesh HSC (Higher Secondary Certificate) as equivalent to **HAVO level** (NOT VWO). This means: HSC qualifies for **universities of applied sciences (HBO)** directly. For **research universities (WO)**, a foundation year or 1+ year of Bangladeshi university study is typically required |
| **HSC + 1 Year University** | Completing HSC + at least 1 year at a recognized Bangladeshi university (e.g., Dhaka University, BUET) generally qualifies for WO bachelor admission |
| **Master's Admission** | 4-year bachelor's degree (e.g., BSc Hons from National University or public university) is accepted. Some universities require minimum 60–70% marks |
| **Financial Proof (Visa)** | Must transfer full financial guarantee of **€13,620** to university bank account (bank statements NOT accepted — full transfer required) |
| **Visa** | MVV + VVR required. University acts as sponsor and applies on your behalf via TEV procedure |
| **Living Cost Proof** | **€932/month** (≈ ৳1,19,296) minimum |

### Pakistan 🇵🇰

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **HSSC Diploma Equivalence** | Nuffic rates Pakistan HSSC (Higher Secondary School Certificate) with **C grades or above** as comparable to **HAVO level**. Similar to Bangladesh — direct HBO admission, WO may require foundation year or 1+ year Pakistani university |
| **HSSC + University** | 2-year FA/FSc + at least 1 year at a recognized Pakistani university qualifies for most WO programmes |
| **Master's Admission** | 4-year bachelor's degree (BS/BE from HEC-recognized university) accepted. 2-year BA/BSc (14 years education) generally NOT sufficient — need 16 years total |
| **PhD Admission** | 4-year bachelor's + 2-year master's (MS/MPhil from HEC-recognized university) |
| **Financial Proof (Visa)** | Must transfer full financial guarantee of **€13,620** to university bank account (same as Bangladesh — full transfer, no bank statements) |
| **Visa** | MVV + VVR required via TEV procedure |

### India 🇮🇳

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **12th Standard Equivalence** | Nuffic rates Indian 12th Standard (CBSE/ISC/State Board) as comparable to **HAVO level** with certain conditions. For WO admission, most universities require 12th Standard + completion of at least 1 year at a recognized Indian university |
| **Direct WO Admission** | Some universities accept 12th Standard with 75%+ marks directly for specific programmes (check per university) |
| **Master's Admission** | 3-year bachelor's degree (e.g., BA/BSc from Indian university) is generally accepted. 4-year BTech/BE degrees are well-recognized. Minimum 60–75% or equivalent CGPA |
| **Financial Proof (Visa)** | Must show proof of **€13,000+** for the first year. For students from Punjab, Jammu & Kashmir: full financial guarantee transfer required (similar to Bangladesh/Pakistan) |
| **Visa** | MVV + VVR required via TEV procedure. University sponsors the application |

### Student Visa (MVV + VVR) — All Three Countries

| Requirement | Details |
|------------|---------|
| **Visa Type** | MVV (entry visa, 90-day sticker) + VVR (residence permit, biometric card) |
| **Application** | University applies on your behalf as recognized sponsor (TEV procedure) |
| **Financial Requirement** | Minimum **€13,000–€13,620/year** proof of funds |
| **Health Insurance** | Mandatory — basic Dutch health insurance (~€130/month) or private international insurance |
| **Processing Time** | 2–3 weeks (IND), but start 3 months before travel |
| **Visa Fee** | ~€210 (MVV+VVR combined) |
| **Orientation Year Visa** | After graduation, eligible for 1-year "zoekjaar" residence permit to find work |

## KEY FACTS ABOUT STUDYING IN THE NETHERLANDS

| Fact | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Language of Instruction** | 2,100+ English-taught programmes (highest in continental Europe) |
| **Academic Year** | September – June (some programmes also start in February) |
| **Grading System** | 1–10 scale (6.0 = pass, 8.0+ = excellent, 10 rarely given) |
| **ECTS System** | Yes — full year = 60 ECTS, bachelor = 180 ECTS, master = 60–120 ECTS |
| **Work While Studying** | Non-EU students: max 16 hours/week (need TWV work permit from employer) OR full-time during June/July/August |
| **Post-Study Work Visa** | "Zoekjaar" (orientation year) — 1 year residence permit to find work after graduation |
| **Application System** | Studielink (mandatory for all Dutch higher education) + university portal |
| **Numerus Fixus** | Some programmes have limited places and use selection/lottery (deadline: 15 January) |
| **Academic Culture** | Problem-Based Learning (PBL) common at Maastricht, EUR; interactive/seminar-based at most universities |
| **Country Population** | ~17.9 million |
| **International Students** | ~122,000 international students (2024-2025) |
